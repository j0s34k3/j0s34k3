## The Core

My work focuses on the translation between scales: understanding how micro-scale interactions govern macro-scale emergent behavior. Whether dealing with the stochastic trajectories of particles, the structure of cosmic observables, or the dynamics of urban networks, the core challenge remains the same: formalizing the mathematical frameworks that map underlying chaos into structured, observable order.

I develop and implement computational models—anchored in non-equilibrium statistical mechanics and accelerated by generative architectures—to explore these boundaries.

## Research Pillars

### I. Deep Space & Stochastic Inference
*   **The Narrative:** Cosmological parameter estimation is historically bottle-necked by the computational cost of traditional sampling. By treating information flow as a stochastic process, we can bypass these limits, transforming simulation from a brute-force calculation into an accelerated, scalable tool.
*   **The Method:** Implementing score-based generative frameworks (Diffusion Models) structured as numeric discretizations of the Fokker-Planck equation to emulate power spectra and accelerate likelihood-free inference.

$$\frac{\partial p}{\partial t} = -\nabla \cdot(\vec{f} p) + D \nabla^2 p$$

### II. Non-Linear Dynamics & Amorphous Structures
*   **The Narrative:** Transport phenomena and energy localization in disordered media lack the clean symmetries of ideal crystals. To understand glass transitions and anomalous relaxation, we must isolate the topological and dynamic features that dictate how energy diffuses through structural chaos.
*   **The Method:** Executing large-scale Molecular Dynamics (MD) simulations to analyze energy transport in Fermi-Pasta-Ulam-Tsingou (FPUT) diatomic chains and structural relaxation in multi-component silicate glasses.

### III. Network Science & Urban Topology
*   **The Narrative:** Modern infrastructure systems are massive, interconnected graphs vulnerable to critical phase transitions. In public transit, a localized perturbation can trigger a cascading collapse; identifying the topological anchors of these networks is vital for systemic resilience.
*   **The Method:** Applying graph theory and structural network analysis to real-world transit data, quantifying vulnerability indices, and mapping propagation dynamics under localized failures.
